How To Pick Up an Apple

Step 1: Have an Apple in Your Hand

If you followed the steps from the “How to Pick Up an Apple” guide, or someone else placed an apple in your hand, you should have this step completed.

Step 2: Find Somewhere to Put the Apple Down

In order to put the apple down, you must first have a place to put it down. It should preferably be somewhere flat, sanitary, not alive, or having ever been alive, unless it is something made of wood, in which case it is okay if it used to be alive.

Step 3: Hold Your Hand Above the Surface

Put your hand, along with the apple, a few inches to a few feet above the surface you wish to place your apple on

Step 4: Lower Your Hand

Move your hand downwards towards the surface until either your hand or your apple are touching it. Make sure not to move your hand too fast, otherwise you may break either your hand, your arm, your apple, your surface, the sound barrier, or the speed of light.

Step 5: Release the Apple

Release your grip from the apple, uncurling your fingers from around it until you can move your hand without moving the apple as well.

Step 6: Move Your Hand Away

Move your hand away from the apple so that your hand is no longer touching it. Congratulations! You have now put down an apple.
